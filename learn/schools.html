The Four Schools Compared

Hanafi · Maliki · Shafi'i · Hanbali

All four Sunni schools of jurisprudence (madhabs) agree on the core principles of Zakat but differ on specific rulings. This diversity reflects the richness of Islamic jurisprudence. All four are considered valid and orthodox, and scholars of each school have traditionally respected the others.

Which school should I follow? Follow the school that is prevalent in your community or the one your trusted scholar advises. If you are unsure, many contemporary scholars recommend erring on the side of caution (e.g. paying Zakat on jewellery even if your school exempts it).

01Overview of Each School

Hanafi

Founded by: Imam Abu Hanifa (d. 767 CE)

Prevalent in: South Asia, Turkey, Central Asia, Balkans, parts of Middle East and North Africa

The largest school globally. Known for rational methodology and inclusive approach to zakatable assets.

Maliki

Founded by: Imam Malik ibn Anas (d. 795 CE)

Prevalent in: North and West Africa, parts of the Gulf

Emphasises the practice of the people of Madinah as a key legal source.

Shafi'i

Founded by: Imam al-Shafi'i (d. 820 CE)

Prevalent in: Southeast Asia, East Africa, Yemen, Egypt, parts of the Levant

Bridges between hadith-based and reason-based approaches. Wrote the first treatise on legal theory.

Hanbali

Founded by: Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (d. 855 CE)

Prevalent in: Saudi Arabia, Qatar, parts of UAE and Gulf

The smallest school. Strictest adherence to hadith texts with less reliance on analogy.

03All Four Schools Are Valid

It is important to remember that all four schools are considered equally valid paths within Sunni Islam. As scholars have noted, the diversity of opinion is a mercy and reflects the depth of Islamic jurisprudence. The truth lies within these four schools, and following any one of them with sincerity fulfils one's obligation.

لَّيْسَ الْبِرَّ أَن تُوَلُّوا وُجُوهَكُمْ قِبَلَ الْمَشْرِقِ وَالْمَغْرِبِ وَلَٰكِنَّ الْبِرَّ مَنْ آمَنَ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ وَالْمَلَائِكَةِ وَالْكِتَابِ وَالنَّبِيِّينَ وَآتَى الْمَالَ عَلَىٰ حُبِّهِ ذَوِي الْقُرْبَىٰ وَالْيَتَامَىٰ وَالْمَسَاكِينَ وَابْنَ السَّبِيلِ وَالسَّائِلِينَ

"Goodness does not consist in turning your face towards East or West. The truly good are those who believe in God and the Last Day, in the angels, the Scripture, and the prophets; who give away some of their wealth, however much they cherish it, to relatives, to orphans, the needy, travellers, and beggars." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ١٧٧Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:177
