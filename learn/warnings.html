Warnings & Virtues

The gravity and beauty of Zakat

The Quran and the Prophetic traditions speak about Zakat with extraordinary force, both in warning against its neglect and in describing its spiritual rewards. Understanding these dimensions transforms Zakat from a mere financial calculation into a profound act of worship.

01Quranic Warnings

وَالَّذِينَ يَكْنِزُونَ الذَّهَبَ وَالْفِضَّةَ وَلَا يُنفِقُونَهَا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ فَبَشِّرْهُم بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ ۝ يَوْمَ يُحْمَىٰ عَلَيْهَا فِي نَارِ جَهَنَّمَ فَتُكْوَىٰ بِهَا جِبَاهُهُمْ وَجُنُوبُهُمْ وَظُهُورُهُمْ ۖ هَٰذَا مَا كَنَزْتُمْ لِأَنفُسِكُمْ فَذُوقُوا مَا كُنتُمْ تَكْنِزُونَ

"And those who hoard gold and silver and spend it not in the way of Allah, give them tidings of a painful punishment. The Day when it will be heated in the fire of Hell and seared therewith will be their foreheads, their flanks, and their backs, and it will be said: 'This is what you hoarded for yourselves. Now taste what you used to hoard.'" القرآن الكريم، سورة التوبة، الآيتان ٣٤-٣٥Quran, At-Tawbah 9:34-35

The Prophet (peace be upon him) clarified that wealth whose Zakat has been paid does not fall under the category of "hoarded" wealth. It is only wealth on which Zakat was withheld that earns this severe warning. In other words, paying Zakat is what separates lawful savings from blameworthy hoarding.

02The Hadith of the Plates of Fire

Abu Hurairah reported that the Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "If any owner of gold or silver does not pay what is due on it, when the Day of Resurrection comes, plates of fire will be beaten out for him. These will be heated in the fire of Hell, and his sides, his forehead, and his back will be cauterized with them. Whenever they cool down, the process is repeated, during a day the length of which would be fifty thousand years, until judgement is pronounced among the servants, and he sees whether his path leads to Paradise or to Hell." Sahih Muslim, Kitab al-Zakat

This hadith describes a punishment so severe and prolonged that it should move every Muslim who possesses wealth above the Nisab to pay their Zakat diligently and completely. The warning applies equally to gold and silver in all forms, whether for personal use or for trade, without distinction.

03The Promise of Purification

خُذْ مِنْ أَمْوَالِهِمْ صَدَقَةً تُطَهِّرُهُمْ وَتُزَكِّيهِم بِهَا وَصَلِّ عَلَيْهِمْ ۖ إِنَّ صَلَاتَكَ سَكَنٌ لَّهُمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ

"Take alms from their wealth in order to purify them and sanctify them with it." القرآن الكريم، سورة التوبة، الآية ١٠٣Quran, At-Tawbah 9:103

The very word "Zakat" means purification. Allah promises that paying Zakat purifies wealth of any spiritual impurity, and sanctifies the giver. There is no promise of purification or growth in wealth until full Zakat is paid. Some scholars have noted that paying Zakat on all gold and silver (including worn jewellery) is the only way to ensure complete purification of one's wealth.

04The Angels' Prayer

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Every day, two angels descend. One of them says: 'O Allah, grant abundance to him who spends in Your cause.' The other says: 'O Allah, bring destruction to him who withholds.'" Bukhari and Muslim

Every single day, the angels invoke Allah for those who give generously and against those who withhold. This is a daily, ongoing supplication tied directly to one's willingness to spend in Allah's cause.

05Wealth Does Not Decrease

"Charity does not in any way decrease wealth." Sahih Muslim

يَمْحَقُ اللَّهُ الرِّبَا وَيُرْبِي الصَّدَقَاتِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ كَفَّارٍ أَثِيمٍ

"Allah destroys riba (usury) and nourishes charities." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ٢٧٦Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:276

This is one of the most counter-intuitive yet consistently affirmed principles in Islam: giving away 2.5% of your wealth does not diminish it. It actually increases it, both through Allah's blessing (barakah) in the remaining wealth and through the tangible economic circulation that strengthens communities.

06The Serpent of Hoarded Wealth

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Whomsoever Allah has bestowed with wealth and he does not pay its Zakat, that wealth will be made to appear before him on the Day of Judgement in the form of a bald serpent with two horns. His neck will be encircled therewith, and it will hold him with its two jawbones and say: 'I am your wealth; I am your hoarded treasure.'" Bukhari and Muslim

The image is vivid and terrifying: the very wealth a person loved and clung to becomes the instrument of their torment, transformed into a serpent that identifies itself as the wealth they refused to purify.

07Abu Bakr's Stand

After the Prophet's death, some tribes refused to pay Zakat while still professing Islam. Abu Bakr, the first Caliph, declared: "By Allah, I will fight anyone who separates prayer from Zakat, for Zakat is the compulsory right to be taken from wealth." This became the Wars of Apostasy (Hurub al-Riddah), in which Abu Bakr treated the refusal to pay Zakat as equivalent to abandoning Islam itself.

The severity of these warnings should not cause despair, but rather motivate careful calculation and prompt payment. Allah is merciful to those who fulfil their obligations sincerely, even if they have made mistakes in the past. If you have missed Zakat in previous years, estimate and pay it as soon as possible.
