Zakat vs Sadaqah

Obligatory duty and voluntary giving

The words "Zakat" and "Sadaqah" are sometimes used interchangeably in Islamic texts, which can lead to confusion. While both involve giving, they are fundamentally different in nature, rules, and scope.

01Key Differences

Aspect Zakat Sadaqah
Nature Obligatory (fard) Voluntary
Rate Fixed: 2.5% on monetary wealth Any amount, no minimum or maximum
Recipients Only the 8 Quranic categories Anyone: Muslim or non-Muslim, rich or poor
Timing After Hawl completes on Nisab Any time, any occasion
Nisab Required Not required
Consequence Sin if neglected; remains as debt No sin if not given; rewarded if given
Form Usually monetary Money, goods, actions, even a smile

02Qard-e-Hasana: The Goodly Loan

One of the most beautiful concepts in the Quran is Qard-e-Hasana, the "goodly loan" to Allah. Despite being the Creator, Owner, and Sustainer of all things, Allah asks His servants to "lend" to Him, promising manifold repayment. The word qard literally means "to cut off," implying that a person cuts away a portion of their wealth and entrusts it to Allah.

This concept appears twelve times across six Quranic verses:

مَّن ذَا الَّذِي يُقْرِضُ اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا فَيُضَاعِفَهُ لَهُ أَضْعَافًا كَثِيرَةً ۚ وَاللَّهُ يَقْبِضُ وَيَبْسُطُ وَإِلَيْهِ تُرْجَعُونَ

"Who is the one who would give Allah a goodly loan so that Allah multiplies it for him many times? Allah withholds and extends, and to Him you are to be returned." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ٢٤٥Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:245

إِن تُقْرِضُوا اللَّهَ قَرْضًا حَسَنًا يُضَاعِفْهُ لَكُمْ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ۚ وَاللَّهُ شَكُورٌ حَلِيمٌ

"If you offer unto Allah a goodly loan, He will amply repay you for it, and will forgive your sins: for Allah is ever Responsive to gratitude and is Forbearing." القرآن الكريم، سورة التغابن، الآية ١٧Quran, At-Taghabun 64:17

Qard-e-Hasana encompasses all forms of voluntary giving: supporting the poor, maintaining orphans and widows, paying the debts of the impoverished, spending on one's family, and lending money to the genuinely needy with the intention of forgiving the debt if they cannot repay.

The Story of Abu Al-Dahdah

When the verse about Qard-e-Hasana was revealed, a companion named Abu Al-Dahdah came to the Prophet (peace be upon him) and asked if it was true that Allah had asked for a goodly loan. When the Prophet confirmed, Abu Al-Dahdah pledged his entire orchard of 600 date palm trees as his loan to Allah. He went to the garden, called his wife Umm Al-Dahdah to come out with her belongings, and handed the entire orchard to the Prophet for distribution to the needy.

03The Reward of Giving

مَّثَلُ الَّذِينَ يُنفِقُونَ أَمْوَالَهُمْ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ كَمَثَلِ حَبَّةٍ أَنبَتَتْ سَبْعَ سَنَابِلَ فِي كُلِّ سُنبُلَةٍ مِّائَةُ حَبَّةٍ ۗ وَاللَّهُ يُضَاعِفُ لِمَن يَشَاءُ ۗ وَاللَّهُ وَاسِعٌ عَلِيمٌ

"The parable of those who spend their possessions for the sake of Allah is that of a grain out of which grow seven ears, in every ear a hundred grains: for Allah grants manifold increase unto whom He wills." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ٢٦١Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:261

The Prophet (peace be upon him) taught several principles about the reward of charity:

"I and the one who patronises an orphan would be like two joined fingers in Paradise." Bukhari
"Charity does not in any way decrease wealth." Muslim
"The one who provides clothes to a fellow Muslim in need, Allah will grant him garments of Paradise. The one who feeds a Muslim in hunger, Allah will bestow heavenly fruits upon him." Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi

04What Enhances Charity

Sincerity (Ikhlas): Giving purely for the sake of Allah, not for recognition or praise. The Quran teaches that even one penny spent with sincere intention brings a reward up to seven hundred times the original amount, or even more.

Secrecy: Giving privately, so that the left hand does not know what the right hand has given. This preserves the dignity of the recipient and purifies the giver's intention.

Kindness: A kind word that accompanies charity, or even replaces it when one has nothing to give, is better than charity followed by hurtful behaviour.

05What Invalidates Charity

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُبْطِلُوا صَدَقَاتِكُم بِالْمَنِّ وَالْأَذَىٰ كَالَّذِي يُنفِقُ مَالَهُ رِئَاءَ النَّاسِ وَلَا يُؤْمِنُ بِاللَّهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الْآخِرِ

"O you who believe! Do not deprive your charitable deeds of all worth by stressing your own benevolence and hurting the feelings of the needy, as does he who spends his wealth only to be seen and praised by men." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ٢٦٤Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:264

Three things can render charity spiritually worthless:

Show-off (Riya): Giving in order to be seen, praised, or recognised as generous.

Self-aggrandisement (Mann): Reminding the recipient of your favour, or boasting about your generosity.

Condescension (Adha): Looking down on the recipient, being sarcastic, or causing them emotional hurt.

06Giving During Hardship

الَّذِينَ يُنفِقُونَ فِي السَّرَّاءِ وَالضَّرَّاءِ وَالْكَاظِمِينَ الْغَيْظَ وَالْعَافِينَ عَنِ النَّاسِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ يُحِبُّ الْمُحْسِنِينَ

"Those who spend in time of plenty and in time of hardship, and hold in check their anger, and pardon their fellow men, because Allah loves the doers of good." القرآن الكريم، سورة آل عمران، الآية ١٣٤Quran, Aal-e-Imran 3:134

When asked about the best form of charity, the Prophet (peace be upon him) replied: "Spending in the condition when you are healthy and full of life, when you are afraid of becoming poor and desire to be prosperous." Giving when it is easy carries less spiritual weight than giving when it requires real sacrifice.
