Understanding Nisab

The minimum threshold of wealth

01What is Nisab?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes obligatory. Think of it as a financial floor: if your total zakatable assets are below this threshold, you owe nothing. If they meet or exceed it, you pay 2.5% on the entire zakatable amount, not just the portion above the line.

This is an important distinction. Nisab is a gateway, not a deductible. Either you cross it and owe Zakat on everything, or you do not cross it and owe nothing. There is no partial obligation.

Why Nisab Exists

Nisab prevents those who are themselves in need from being obligated to give. It ensures that only people with genuine surplus wealth contribute, and helps the genuinely poor qualify as recipients rather than donors.

02The Two Standards

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) established the Nisab at two specific amounts from the currencies of his time: 20 gold dinars or 200 silver dirhams.

Gold Nisab

20 Gold Dinars

All Schools 85 grams

Al-Azhar standard — recognised by all four schools

🥈

Silver Nisab

200 Silver Dirhams

All Schools 595 grams

The difference in gram weights between schools stems from different scholarly interpretations of the exact weight of the gold dinar and silver dirham. Scholars have not disagreed on the number of coins — 20 and 200 respectively — but on how much each coin weighed, and these interpretations have produced slightly different gram figures across traditions.

Contemporary scholars have given fatwas permitting the use of either the gold or the silver standard. Depending on which you apply, your Nisab threshold will be significantly higher (gold) or lower (silver). Both are valid positions with scholarly backing.

03Gold or Silver: Which Standard?

Because gold and silver prices have diverged dramatically since the Prophet's time (in his era, the two Nisab values were roughly equivalent in purchasing power), using gold or silver today produces very different thresholds. As of early 2026, the gold Nisab is approximately $14,000+ while the silver Nisab is approximately $1,500-1,800.

This means the choice of standard significantly affects who is obligated to pay:

School Preferred Standard Reasoning
Hanafi Silver (lower threshold) More people pay Zakat, more funds reach the poor. Uses the lower of the two values to maximize benefit.
Maliki Gold (higher threshold) Gold better reflects modern purchasing power and stable wealth.
Shafi'i Allows both Individual choice, though many Shafi'i scholars lean toward gold.
Hanbali Gold (higher threshold) Similar reasoning to Maliki: practical relevance in modern contexts.

Practical rule (Hanafi): If you only own gold, use the gold Nisab. If you have a mixture of assets (cash, gold, silver, investments), use the silver Nisab as the baseline, since it is the lower and more inclusive threshold.

04How Nisab is Calculated

The calculation is straightforward:

Gold Nisab = 85g × current gold price per gram
Silver Nisab = 595g × current silver price per gram

For example, if gold is trading at $166 per gram: 85 × $166 = $14,110 (gold Nisab). If silver is at $2.80 per gram: 595 × $2.80 = $1,666 (silver Nisab).

05The Seed Date

The first day your wealth reaches or exceeds the Nisab is called your seed date. This is the starting point of your personal Zakat year (Hawl). From this date, one full lunar year (354 days) must pass before Zakat becomes due.

If you cannot remember the exact date your wealth first reached Nisab, make your best estimate. If no estimate is possible, simply fix a date (many choose the start of Ramadan) and calculate consistently on that date every year.

The concept of the Hawl (lunar year) is explored in detail on our Understanding the Hawl page.

06Nisab for Other Types of Wealth

Agricultural Produce

Agricultural produce has its own Nisab, separate from gold and silver. The majority of scholars (following Imams Abu Yusuf, Muhammad, Malik, Shafi'i, and Ahmad) set this at 5 Wasaq, approximately 653 kg of grain or dates. However, Imam Abu Hanifa held that there is no minimum Nisab for agricultural Zakat (Ushr); it is payable on every amount of produce, regardless of quantity.

Livestock

Livestock has specific Nisab thresholds: camels become zakatable starting at 5 head, cattle at 30 head, and sheep/goats at 40 head. Each has its own payment schedule detailed in classical manuals.

Mining and Treasure (Rikaz)

Treasure found underground has no Nisab requirement and no Hawl. Zakat (at 20% rather than 2.5%) is due immediately upon discovery.
