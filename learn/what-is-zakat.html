What is Zakat?

The Third Pillar of Islam

01The Meaning of Zakat

The word Zakat comes from the Arabic root zakā (زكا), which carries several interconnected meanings: growth, increase, purification, and blessing. This is no coincidence. Scholars note that paying Zakat simultaneously purifies a person's wealth and causes it to grow, both materially and spiritually.

In the terminology of Islamic law (Shari'ah), Zakat is defined as a specified obligatory portion taken from specific types of wealth and given to designated categories of recipients, with the intention of worship and obedience to Allah.

The Hanafi Definition

Zakat is the act of transferring ownership (Tamlik) of a specified portion of one's wealth to a qualifying recipient, as prescribed by Allah. The key word here is transferring ownership, meaning the recipient must be made the full owner of the Zakat given to them.

Zakat also applies to livestock and agricultural produce (grains, dates, and raisins), each with their own thresholds and rules. This site focuses on Zakat al-Mal (monetary wealth: gold, silver, cash, and trade goods). For rulings on animals and crops, please consult a qualified scholar or mufti directly.

02A Pillar of Islam

Zakat is the third of the five pillars of Islam, placed immediately after the declaration of faith (Shahada) and prayer (Salah). Its importance is such that the Quran refers to Zakat in 82 separate verses and pairs it directly with Salah on 32 occasions.

وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ ۚ وَمَا تُقَدِّمُوا لِأَنفُسِكُم مِّنْ خَيْرٍ تَجِدُوهُ عِندَ اللَّهِ ۗ إِنَّ اللَّهَ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ بَصِيرٌ

"Establish regular prayer and give Zakat, and whatever good you put forward for yourselves, you will find it with Allah." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ١١٠Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:110

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) counted Zakat among the five pillars of Islam. Whoever denies its obligation has rejected a fundamental tenet of the faith. Whoever acknowledges it but refuses to pay it commits a grave sin, and the Zakat remains as a debt upon them.

03Inner and Outer Dimensions

Scholars describe Zakat as having both an outer dimension and an inner dimension:

The Outer Dimension

Zakat serves as a filter that purifies a believer's wealth, removing impurities so that the remaining wealth may increase in a manner pleasing to Allah. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Charity does not in any way decrease wealth." (Muslim)

The Inner Dimension

Zakat suppresses the ego and quells destructive tendencies such as greed, jealousy, and miserliness. It trains the soul to detach from material wealth and to see one's possessions as a trust (amanah) from Allah, not as personal entitlements.

خُذْ مِنْ أَمْوَالِهِمْ صَدَقَةً تُطَهِّرُهُمْ وَتُزَكِّيهِم بِهَا وَصَلِّ عَلَيْهِمْ ۖ إِنَّ صَلَاتَكَ سَكَنٌ لَّهُمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ سَمِيعٌ عَلِيمٌ

"Take alms from their wealth in order to purify them and sanctify them with it, and invoke Allah for them. Verily, your invocations are a source of comfort for them." القرآن الكريم، سورة التوبة، الآية ١٠٣Quran, At-Tawbah 9:103

04Historical Context

Zakat was initially commanded in Makkah during the early period of Islam, as deduced by the great Quranic commentator Ibn Kathir from Surah al-Muzammil, which is among the earliest chapters revealed. However, in this initial period, no specific amounts or thresholds (Nisab) were set. Muslims simply spent a major portion of whatever remained after fulfilling their needs in the cause of Allah.

The specification of Nisab thresholds and the determination of exact rates came after the emigration (Hijrah) to Madinah. This is when Zakat took its structured, legal form that we follow today.

Abu Bakr's Enforcement

After the Prophet's passing, some Arab tribes refused to pay Zakat while still claiming to be Muslim. Abu Bakr, the first Caliph, declared: "By Allah, I will fight anyone who separates prayer from Zakat." He launched what became known as the Wars of Apostasy (Hurub al-Riddah), underscoring that Zakat is not optional. It is as central to Islam as prayer itself.

05Benefits of Zakat

While the primary purpose of Zakat is obedience to Allah's command, regardless of any worldly benefit, Islam teaches that paying Zakat yields tremendous blessings:

Purification and growth of wealth. Allah says in the Quran:

يَمْحَقُ اللَّهُ الرِّبَا وَيُرْبِي الصَّدَقَاتِ ۗ وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ كَفَّارٍ أَثِيمٍ

"Allah destroys riba (usury) and nourishes charities." القرآن الكريم، سورة البقرة، الآية ٢٧٦Quran, Al-Baqarah 2:276

The Prophet (peace be upon him) taught that when a person pays Zakat, the angels pray: "O Allah! Grant abundance to him who spends in Your cause."

Social welfare. Zakat functions as a structured wealth redistribution mechanism, ensuring that surplus wealth reaches those in genuine need, preventing the concentration of wealth among the few.

Spiritual discipline. Regular giving trains the soul to be generous, grateful, and detached from materialism. It is a reminder that all wealth ultimately belongs to Allah and that we are merely trustees.

06Zakat vs Sadaqah

The words "Zakat" and "Sadaqah" are sometimes used interchangeably in Islamic texts (including in certain Quranic verses), which can cause confusion. However, they are distinct:

Aspect Zakat Sadaqah
Obligation Mandatory (fard) Voluntary
Rate Fixed at 2.5% for monetary wealth Any amount
Recipients Only the 8 Quranic categories Anyone in need
Timing After Hawl completes Any time
To non-Muslims Generally not permitted Permitted and encouraged
Nisab required Yes, must reach minimum threshold No minimum
Consequence of neglect Sin, remains as debt No sin

For a deeper exploration of voluntary charity, including the concept of Qard-e-Hasana (the Goodly Loan to Allah), see our dedicated page on Zakat vs Sadaqah.

07The Three-Word Rule

The entire system of Zakat can be summarised in three words:

Nisab

Reaching the minimum threshold of wealth

Hawl

Completing one full lunar year of ownership

Tamlik

Transferring ownership to the eligible recipient

If your wealth meets the Nisab, has been held for a full Hawl, and you give it with Tamlik (direct ownership transfer) to an eligible person, your Zakat is fulfilled.
